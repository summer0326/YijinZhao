PORTFOLIO

Experience of London: Culture and Social

Social and cultural opportunities are important factors when evaluating city liveability. A vital aspect of liveability is an individual’s perspective, which includes citizens’ subjective evaluation of the quality of both tangible (public infrastructures) and intangible features (sense of place).

Societal and cultural interactions are intangible features that bring individuals a sense of place and strengthen social cohesion. Daily events create a medium which connects people to their city. It allows for interactions between not only people but also people and place. There are many types of events, and individuals with the same hobbies or skills can gather to share knowledge and interact socially.

In these visualisations, we explore the liveability of London through a week’s worth of social and cultural experiences. We investigate these experiences through weekly public events throughout the city.

Mapbox GL is used to visualise the spatial data through maps, Echarts to build the word cloud map, and D3, Highcharts, and AnyChart were used to build interactive charts, all of which combined to create the story of events in London.

Travel to work-London (demo)

This map visualization is based on HTML website, with using Mapbox Studio and the JavaScript library Mapbox GL, to display traffic counts and commuting patterns by users’ interactivities. The other tools of QGIS and Python are also used in processing and transforming the original data to be available in spatial visualization. Interactions between users and the map are achieved with event response procedure which involved 4 cartographic layers with the time span of first layer from 2010 to 2017. Through clicking the layer on the top, users can change the layer from total traffic counts, travel to work by public transports and by bike, to ‘fly to’ a specific zoom range of bicycle flows in central area. Other interactive actions include popup with mouseover and dragging the time bar.
